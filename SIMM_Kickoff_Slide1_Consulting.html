Background: Regulatory Evolution, Definition Matrix, and Problem Statement

SIMM Validation Kick-off | Phase 1 Context Page | Consulting Version (Single Slide)
Project Baseline: ISDA SIMM v2.8+2506 (effective 6 Dec 2025), assessed against HKMA SPM CR-G-14 supervisory expectations for implementation integrity, data governance, and audit traceability.

A) Regulatory Timeline (Why this validation is mandatory)

  • 2008–2009: Global financial crisis exposed bilateral OTC counterparty risk and collateral insufficiency.
  • 2011–2013: BCBS-IOSCO introduced global margin standards for non-centrally cleared derivatives.
  • 2015–2020: UMR phased implementation across major jurisdictions; IM governance became supervisory focus.
  • 2016 onward: ISDA SIMM adopted as the sensitivity-based industry standard for IM calculation.
  • 2023–2025: Higher supervisory scrutiny on model version governance, parameter updates, and data lineage.
  • Current: Institutions are expected to prove clause-level compliance, not only produce IM numbers.

C) Problems this framework must solve

  1. Regulatory Defensibility Gap: Calculation output is not equivalent to regulatory compliance evidence.
  2. Implementation Integrity Risk: Wrong SIMM version/parameter/correlation logic may remain undetected.
  3. Sensitivity Transmission Risk: Correct pricing does not guarantee correct Delta/Vega/Curvature mapping.
  4. Data Lineage Risk: Trade and market data breaks distort IM at component and total levels.
  5. Governance Sustainability Risk: One-off testing cannot satisfy recurring supervisory/audit expectations.
